Roundup of ISY 2019

FRIDAY, AUGUST 30

8/30/2019 0 Comments

On our fifth and final day of Hicksville ISY, our session started off with a colorful experiment. Students began by pouring milk into a container and adding spots of food coloring. When they touched the center of the milk with their finger, the milk and food coloring remained stationary. However, after dipping their fingers in dish soap and then proceeding to insert their finger in the milk once again, students observed that the food coloring quickly traveled to the sides of the plate, creating a vibrant burst of colors. Why does this happen? The surface tension of the milk is reduced when dish soap is added due to the dissolving of the milk’s fat molecules by the soap. However, the surface tension of the milk outside the soap drop is higher so the milk’s surface is pulled away from that spot. The food coloring moves with the milk’s surface as the density of the food coloring is lower than the density of milk. Students were amazed to see the rapid movement of colors with just the touch of a finger! Afterwards, students indulged in making colorful mosaics of milk and food coloring.

For our second lesson, we decided to end the program with a tasty summer treat- ice cream! However, this was no ordinary ice cream. Students were able to make their own frozen delights with the use of chemistry! By adding milk, vanilla extract and sugar in a sandwich bag, students created the basic mixture for their ice cream. Next, the sandwich bag was placed in an even bigger ziplock bag which contained ice and salt. The students then proceeded to shake the large bag vigorously. The salt added to the ice lowers the melting point of the ice, just as it does when we add salt to icy roads. In order for the ice to melt, it must absorb heat from the sandwich bag, cooling the ingredients in the sandwich bag and ultimately creating ice cream! Using only simple household items, we were able to observe chemistry in action as well as enjoy a delicious dessert. Students were surprised to see such simple materials turn into ice cream with only a few shakes!

ISY 2019 was a huge success! Whether it was meeting a new friend, discovering a newfound interest in science or just a nice way to spend the summer, we hope that students took something from this program. It has been an honor and a phenomenal experience being ISY instructors this year and having the opportunity to meet these wonderful students with brilliant minds. Until next time!
